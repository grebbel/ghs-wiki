# Three professionals in 2050

## Is "LinkedIn" still a thing?

Yes, but only structurally. The brand persists — Microsoft never sold it, the network-effect moat held — but the product bifurcated around 2034 under regulatory and competitive pressure. The professional identity infrastructure of 2050 has two layers:

- **The narrative layer**, what humans actually read. Short, prose, human-curated. People still keep them because senior leaders making final calls open them. LinkedIn the brand survived here.
- **The Vantage layer**, what agents query. Open protocol descended from the Globerson et al. 2026 measurement work; verifies durable-skills assessment scores, harness-portfolio cryptographic signatures, evaluation-expertise certifications, and work-trace excerpts. Most hiring, contracting, and partnership decisions in 2050 flow through this layer; agents talk to agents (per Karpathy's 2026 prediction) and only escalate to humans for the consequential calls.

The three profiles below show both layers. The format is now standard across the EU's *Professional Identity Directive* (2038) and adopted in the AU and ASEAN by 2042; the US ratified an interoperable equivalent in 2046.

## Reading the three profiles together

Three observations the profiles make legible that the prior essay only argued in the abstract.

First, the **route into a successful 2050 career** is no longer routed through a 2020s-style elite credential. Adaeze is from a corporate-banking background; Linh trained at a regional polytechnic with an explicit neurodivergence-inclusive curriculum; Kofi went through a community learning collective in Nairobi. What unifies them is not pedigree but evidence — Vantage scores, ratified specifications, harness configurations in the open registry, work-trace signatures auditors can verify. The Brynjolfsson 2025 canaries warning was about the *codified-knowledge* entry pipeline narrowing; the durable-skills + evaluation-expertise pipeline that replaced it turned out to be more porous, not less.

Second, the **agent-of-record convention** is doing a lot of work here. By 2050, every working professional has one. For Linh and Kofi the agent is partly an accommodation infrastructure — Linh's mediates synchronous social load; Kofi's runs KSL translation across all modalities — but for everyone the agent is also the layer that makes the Vantage profile queryable. The 2026 wiki recorded Karpathy's prediction that *"I'll have my agent talk to your agent to figure out some of the details of our meetings"*; the 2050 form of that prediction is that **most professional matchmaking and coordination happens at the agent layer**, with humans only opening the narrative-layer profile when a consequential decision approaches.

Third, the **expert-as-evaluator track** that Kiron and Schrage named in 2026 turns out to be the dominant mid-career path. All three profiles intersect with it: Adaeze runs evaluation expertise as a strategic capability for her firm; Linh leads a team that builds evaluation rubrics as a service; Kofi is on the explicit evaluation-expertise track from year one. The wiki had recorded this as a strong claim; in the 2050 biographies it reads as the matter-of-course default career structure for anyone whose work is consequential. The roles called *"producer"* in 2026 — first-pass drafter, junior analyst, junior coder — survived only as agent fleets. The roles called *"evaluator"* — recognising what *not perfect* actually means in a domain — became where the durable career sits.

What is genuinely missing from these CVs, against a 2026 yardstick, is the language of *raw output volume*. None of the three profiles foregrounds how many reports written, how many tickets closed, how many lines of code shipped. By 2050 those metrics have moved into the "what your agent did" column and dropped out of the human profile entirely. What stays in the human's CV is what the human uniquely contributed: spec-design taste, evaluation expertise, ratified judgment, captured learning that compounded for the firm. That is not an aesthetic choice; it is the labour-market consequence of the Polanyi tacit-dimension breach the wiki recorded in 2026 — once tacit knowledge about *production* became inferable from behavioural traces, the durable human contribution moved up one layer to *judgment about what production should serve*.

Whether LinkedIn (or its descendants) deserves to host all of this is a separate question. The 2026 wiki noted that "software choices are workforce choices"; in 2050 the choice of professional-identity infrastructure is one of the most consequential workforce-policy decisions a society makes. The EU made one set of choices in 2038; the US, ten years later, made another. Adaeze, Linh, and Kofi each carry both standards.
